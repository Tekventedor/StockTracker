Stock Market Glossary

A-C

Ask Price
The lowest price a seller is willing to accept for a security.
Bear Market
A market condition where prices are falling and negative sentiment prevails.
Bid Price
The highest price a buyer is willing to pay for a security.
Bull Market
A market condition where prices are rising and positive sentiment prevails.
Capital Gain
The profit made from selling a security at a higher price than its purchase price.

D-F

Dividend
A portion of a company's earnings distributed to shareholders.
EPS (Earnings Per Share)
A company's profit divided by the number of outstanding shares.
ETF (Exchange Traded Fund)
A type of investment fund that trades on stock exchanges like individual stocks.
Futures
Contracts to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price at a specified time in the future.

G-I

Growth Stock
A stock that is expected to grow at an above-average rate compared to the market.
Index
A statistical measure of change in a securities market.
IPO (Initial Public Offering)
The first sale of stock by a company to the public.

M-P

Market Cap
The total value of a company's outstanding shares.
P/E Ratio
Price-to-Earnings ratio, a valuation metric for stocks.
Portfolio
A collection of investments held by an individual or institution.
